Gorillaz App

Project information

  • Category: UX Design Case Study
  • Course: Principles of UX/UI
  • Project date: Fall 2024
  • Full Report:: Gorillaz Case Study
  • Skills: Figma, UX Design, UX Research, Usability Testing, Wireframing

Project Description

This project was done throughout the semester in collaboration with 3 fellow classmates. We were all remote students using Slack, Google Meet, and Figma in order to all participate together through the process. During this project, I was able to learn and apply hands on experience through every aspect of the UX Design process.

Case Study Overview

Our goal was to create an app that was easy to use, targeting fans who want quick access to band content, tour dates, and merchandise. We designed the app based on the UX/UI principles we learned throughout the course. The project was completed remotely over 12 weeks as part of our Principles of UX/UI course.

Problem Statement

Gorillaz fans lack a dedicated platform to easily discover and share music, access exclusive content, and connect with other fans in one place.

Research

  • Conducted user interviews and surveys
  • Performed heuristic evaluations and competitive analysis
  • Created user personas and empathy maps

Ideation

We used brainstorming sessions and affinity mapping to generate ideas, ultimately choosing a card-based layout for content discovery and a tabbed navigation structure for clarity.

Design

  • Low-fidelity wireframes and user flows built in Figma
  • High-fidelity mockups with custom illustrations
  • Consistent branding applied using a dark, atmospheric color palette

Testing & Iteration

We conducted two rounds of usability testing via remote sessions. Based on feedback, we improved button visibility, reduced cognitive load, and added a mini player feature for continuous music access.

Outcome

We redesigned the app from the ground up, creating a modern, intuitive interface with streamlined navigation. Our user testing showed increased satisfaction and faster task completion, validating our design choices. You can view our final prototype above or open it in Figma.

Reflection

This project was my first encounter with UX/UI. The project was done throughout the semester, so I was able to really take my time and gain an understanding of what UX/UI was. The biggest thing that I learned during the project is the amount of resources that are available to guide you into a great project. Whether it’s creating a user persona and back story or diving deeper into Figma, there is a tool to help you.
